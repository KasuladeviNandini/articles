How coronavirus is affecting animals?

Coordinate your response

Has representatives from support functions including HR, IT, purchasing, procurement, security and legal and is split across different regions and time zones (where relevant). Develops or implements a business continuity plan, taking into account relevant legal and regulatory obligations and the issues we set out below. Prioritises effectively and manages potentially conflicting imperatives.

Manage the impact on the workforce

Monitor local Governmental and World Health Organisation advice and notify/remind staff of recommendations not to travel to particular areas and of measures to help prevent the spread of Covid-19. Introduce policies prohibiting or limiting business travel, particularly to high-risk areas, and consider alternative communication solutions (eg video-conferencing).

Consider insolvency risks

  1. Finding alternative suppliers. Tightening credit terms and including retention of title clauses until payment has been made. Renewed efforts to collect any payment arrears.

  2. The impact of other companies in your group going into insolvency, in particular, your access to vital assets (IP, IT, employees etc) and whether it is still appropriate to exercise group treasury/cash sweep mechanisms

Manage financial arrangements

  1. Financial ratios – are these adversely affected by historic or forecast loss of operational revenue or changes in asset valuations?

  2. Event of Default – is the company in a default situation and at risk of acceleration or of credit support being called or security being enforced?

  3. Material Adverse Effect provisions – will these be triggered?
